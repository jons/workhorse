the wise student hears of the tao and practices it diligently.<br><br>the average student hears of the tao and gives it thought now and again.<br><br>the foolish student hears of the tao and laughs aloud.<br><br>if there were no laughter, the tao would not be what it is.<br><br><br><br>hence it is said:<br><br>the bright path seems dim;<br><br>going forward seems like retreat;<br><br>the easy way seems hard;<br><br>the highest virtue seems empty;<br><br>great purity seems sullied;<br><br>a wealth of virtue seems inadequate;<br><br>the strength of virtue seems frail;<br><br>real virtue seems unreal;<br><br>the perfect square has no corners;<br><br>great talents ripen late;<br><br>the highest notes are hard to hear;<br><br>the greatest form has no shape;<br><br>the tao is hidden and without name.<br><br>the tao alone nourishes and brings everything to fulfillment.
